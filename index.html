About

I have been programming computers since I was 14 when my poor parents gave into my constant nagging and bought me a Tandy 'Trash' TRS-80. I have been programming with .NET since Microsoft released the first alpha in 2000. From 1998 I have worked as freelance software engineer in more teams and on more projects than I care to remember. I started my blog 'Code Rant' in 1997, and have been a regular speaker at developer events both in the UK and Europe, and have been a guest on some popular developer podcasts including DotNetRocks and the European Virtual ALT NET. I also run a monthly .NET user group, Brighton ALT NET. If you're in the area, come along. I keep an office in Brighton and live in the beautiful medieval town of Lewes, just a few miles from the south coast, with my wife and two kids.
